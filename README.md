# Educate MW - Digital Learning Platform for Malawi 🇲🇼

Educate MW is a comprehensive, AI-powered digital learning platform designed specifically for students in Malawi preparing for their MSCE (Malawi School Certificate of Education) exams. This application bridges the gap in educational resources by providing high-quality study materials, video lessons, and an interactive AI tutor accessible to everyone.

## 🚀 Key Features

- **AI Study Teacher**: A personalized AI tutor powered by Google Gemini, trained to help students with complex subjects like Biology, Mathematics, and Physics.
- **Offline Learning**: Students can download past papers, notes, and lessons for offline study, crucial for areas with limited internet connectivity.
- **Student Forum**: A vibrant community where students can share study tips, ask questions, and collaborate on MSCE preparation.
- **Resource Library**: A vast collection of MANEB-aligned past papers, marking schemes, and video explanations.
- **Interactive Quizzes**: Gamified learning with subject-specific quizzes to test knowledge and track progress.
- **Admin Dashboard**: A robust management system for educators to upload resources, track student engagement, and manage the platform.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- **Frontend**: [Next.js 15](https://nextjs.org/) (App Router), [React](https://reactjs.org/), [TypeScript](https://www.typescriptlang.org/)
- **Styling**: [Tailwind CSS](https://tailwindcss.com/) for a modern, responsive mobile-first UI.
- **Animations**: [Framer Motion](https://www.framer.com/motion/) for smooth, professional transitions.
- **Backend/Database**: [Firebase](https://firebase.google.com/) (Firestore, Authentication) for real-time data and secure user management.
- **AI Integration**: [Google Gemini API](https://ai.google.dev/) for the intelligent study assistant.
- **Icons**: [Lucide React](https://lucide.dev/)

## 📱 Mobile-First Design

The application is meticulously designed with a mobile-first approach, ensuring a seamless experience on smartphones, which are the primary devices for most students in Malawi.
